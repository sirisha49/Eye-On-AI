Question
Why is the sky blue?
Answer
The sky appears blue due to a phenomenon called Rayleigh scattering. When sunlight, which contains all visible wavelengths, passes through the Earth's atmosphere, it encounters gas molecules. These molecules scatter the shorter (blue) wavelengths of light much more than the longer (red) wavelengths. As a result, blue light is dispersed in all directions and reaches our eyes from every part of the sky, giving it a blue appearance. This scattering effect is inversely proportional to the fourth power of the wavelength, which is why blue light (~450nm) scatters roughly 5.5 times more than red light (~700nm).
